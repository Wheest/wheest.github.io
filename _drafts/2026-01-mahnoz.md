**What was your research topic about?**

I looked at the gender toll of policing on the health and well-being of female
police officers in the UK.

**Who were you working with for that?**

I was working with my supervisors and the wider team that I engaged with. My
primary supervisor was based at the School of Health and Wellbeing and my second

It was quite an interdisciplinary project. I was based within these two schools,

Outside of the school, I worked closely with the Scottish Women's Development

Other than them, there were some broader groups like the Scottish Institute for

**In brief, what were the main findings of your thesis?**

I would say the main findings were that the health and well-being of female
police officers cannot be understood as a single piece of work. Rather, I would
describe it as a puzzle. By doing all these different parts, I got a closer

social aspect of health working one way, the mental health aspect another way,
the physical aspect there. They're all intertwined.

Another thing I found was that all of these issuesâ€”the culture or the structural

there is actually organisational injustice, which is controlling how it impacts
overall health.

Lastly, I found that social support, especially workplace social support, plays
a huge role in our lives, which we often overlook.

**It seems these are very interconnected, complex problems. One can't just say
one thing is solely responsible. Nevertheless, would you say the experience

polis[^1] is stressful"? How much can be seen through a gendered lens?**

Every organisation or institution within a society, including families, is a
reflection of the existing society itself. When I was talking to those police

There's a scholar with a famous theory called "Gendered Organisations"â€”her name

those ideas have also changed. But still now, though we keep saying that men and
women are equal in our society, that's not quite the case.

**Is it your impression that police in the UK have more of a "boys' club"

Definitely they had their own boys' club issues. They had cultures where women
wouldn't be invited to the informal parties or after-work gatherings, or they'd
always assume that if you have children, you must need childcare...
